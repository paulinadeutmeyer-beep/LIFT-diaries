Enter
The elevator holds the smell,
of cold,
sour metal,
and of used air.
Doors drag, slowly, hesitate—
then close.
It's too close
to choose otherwise.
A thin line of air
moves between the panels.
I pull my jacket in.
A small, practiced gesture.
We look away. At numbers.
At surfaces.
At anything
that refuses to look back.
A sleeve brushes my hand.
Not enough to react.
Too much to ignore.
The moment stays there,
unresolved.
The cabin tightens without moving.
Metal arguing with gravity.
Then—
a shift.
Not ambition.
Just mechanics.
We rise.
Our breathing enters the space.
There is no distance left,
for it to disappear into.
We wait. Not together.
Just at the same time.
A sound cuts through—
clean, misplaced.
The doors separate.
We step out
almost in sync,
but not quite.
The space closes behind us.
Ready for the next.
